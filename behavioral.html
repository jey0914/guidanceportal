Understanding Peer Support & Social Skills

What is Peer Support?

Peer support is the process of giving and receiving help from people who share similar experiences, challenges, or backgrounds. It's based on the understanding that people who have faced similar situations can offer unique insights, empathy, and practical advice that others might not be able to provide.

For students, peer support creates a sense of belonging, reduces feelings of isolation, and provides opportunities to learn from others who understand what you're going through. It's about building a community where everyone can thrive together.

Why Social Skills Matter

  • Better Relationships: Build stronger, more meaningful connections with others
  • Communication: Express yourself clearly and understand others better
  • Leadership: Develop confidence to lead and inspire others
  • Academic Success: Collaborate effectively on group projects and presentations
